Supatra Daan

The Highest Daan Is Given Where It Creates The Highest Impact

In Sanatan Dharma, the highest form of daan is Supatra Daan — giving to a deserving person, cause or mission. Wealth offered for Dharma, seva and upliftment becomes immortal.

Karna, Bali and Vikramaditya are remembered not only for what they possessed, but for what they gave. Their names outlasted their kingdoms.
✓
A leader's true legacy is not in riches but in generosity.
✓
A visionary's success is not in knowledge alone but in its selfless use.
✓
A true giver is remembered through the lives transformed by their daan.

Everything You Need To Know

Is my donation eligible for 80G tax deduction?+
Yes. Donations to Antar Yog Foundation are eligible for tax deduction under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act. You will receive a formal receipt and 80G certificate for eligible contributions. [Insert PAN and 80G details here.]
How do I know my daan is being used properly?+
Every contribution is directed towards specific seva initiatives. Donors above a certain level receive periodic updates on how their daan is being used. We also provide project-wise accountability for dedicated contributions.
Can I dedicate my daan in someone's name?+
Yes. You can dedicate your daan in memory of ancestors, in the name of your family, for your children's progress, as a birthday or anniversary contribution, or as a sankalp for Dharma, prosperity and inner growth.
What are the payment options?+
You can contribute through online payment (UPI, net banking, debit/credit card), direct bank transfer (NEFT/RTGS) or offline methods (cash, cheque or in-person). [Insert bank and UPI details here.]
Can I set up a recurring monthly daan?+
Yes. You can choose One-Time, Monthly or Annual frequency for your contribution. Monthly seva commitment helps sustain meals, welfare, sadhana, healing and outreach consistently.
