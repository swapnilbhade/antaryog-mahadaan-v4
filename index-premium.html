The Vedic Way Of Giving

Give with OM TAT SAT bhav

As described in the Bhagavad Gita, daan offered with the right bhav becomes spiritually powerful.

OM

Seeing the Divine in the recipient.

TAT

Offering selflessly, without ego or attachment.

SAT

Giving for the true welfare and upliftment of others.

When daan is offered with this bhav, it purifies wealth, uplifts the receiver and returns as blessings to the giver.

Supatra Daan

The highest daan is given where it creates the highest impact

In Sanatan Dharma, the highest form of daan is Supatra Daan — giving to a deserving person, cause or mission. Wealth offered for Dharma, seva and upliftment becomes immortal.

Karna, Bali and Vikramaditya are remembered not only for what they possessed, but for what they gave. Their names outlasted their kingdoms.
—A leader's true legacy is not in riches but in generosity.
—A visionary's success is not in knowledge alone but in its selfless use.
—A true giver is remembered through the lives transformed by their daan.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know

Is my donation eligible for 80G tax deduction?+
Yes. Donations to Antar Yog Foundation are eligible for tax deduction under Section 80G of the Income Tax Act. You will receive a formal receipt and 80G certificate for eligible contributions.
How do I know my daan is being used properly?+
Every contribution is directed towards specific seva initiatives. Donors above a certain level receive periodic updates on how their daan is being used.
Can I dedicate my daan in someone's name?+
Yes. You can dedicate your daan in memory of ancestors, in the name of your family, for your children's progress, or as a sankalp for Dharma, prosperity and inner growth.
What are the payment options?+
You can contribute through online payment (UPI, net banking, debit/credit card), direct bank transfer (NEFT/RTGS) or offline methods (cash, cheque or in-person).
Can I set up a recurring monthly daan?+
Yes. You can choose One-Time, Monthly or Annual frequency for your contribution. Monthly seva commitment helps sustain meals, welfare, sadhana and healing consistently.
